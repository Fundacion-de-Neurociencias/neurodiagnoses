About Neurodiagnoses

Neurodiagnoses is an AI-powered open-source framework for diagnosing and predicting central nervous system (CNS) disorders. It integrates multi-modal data—including genetic, neuroimaging, neurophysiological, and biomarker datasets—with machine learning models to generate structured, explainable diagnostic outputs.

Neurodiagnoses employs a **tridimensional diagnostic model**, structuring its outputs across three axes:

  • Etiology: Genetic, environmental, and molecular contributors to disease.
  • Pathophysiology: Molecular markers, biomarker pathways, and disease mechanisms.
  • Neuroanatomoclinical Presentation: Neuroimaging, clinical symptoms, and physiological markers.

The project also extends into **neurodiagnostic AI research**, focusing on:

  • **CNS Digital Twins**: Integrating omics (genomics, proteomics, lipidomics) with neuroimaging and digital health records.
  • **AI-Driven Risk Stratification**: Personalized prediction models for disease progression and therapeutic response.
  • **Real-Time Clinical Decision Support**: Deploying AI-based diagnostic aids within electronic health record (EHR) systems.

Neurodiagnoses is an open-source initiative with a **dual licensing model (MIT + commercial license)**. While the core framework remains open for research collaboration, an advanced version is planned for clinical, research, and industry applications.

The project is promoted by Fundación de Neurociencias, supporting AI-driven neuroscience research and diagnostics.
