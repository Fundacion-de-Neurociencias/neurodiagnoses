Reimagining Neurological Diagnostics

Diseases of the Central Nervous System (CNS) like Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, or Multiple Sclerosis are incredibly complex. Diagnoses rely on scattered, heterogeneous data—from MRI scans and blood tests to cognitive assessments and genetic profiles.

Neurodiagnoses aims to unify this chaos. We are an open-source project building an AI-assisted diagnostic framework. Our goal is to standardize and process multimodal data to offer clinicians a holistic, probabilistic view, improving accuracy and accelerating the path to personalized treatment.

Key Functionalities

  • Probabilistic Annotation – AI-based diagnostic modeling using probabilistic networks.
  • Tridimensional Annotation – Integration of neuroimaging, genetic, and clinical data.
  • Multi-Model Advanced Annotation – Unified pipeline for comprehensive 3-axis annotation using multiple AI models.
  • Interactive Visualization – User-friendly interfaces for research and clinical applications.
  • Biomarker Prediction – AI-driven estimation of biomarker status and progression.
  • Disease Prediction – Modeling of disease onset, conversion, and progression using ML.

Our 3-Axis Approach

The core of Neurodiagnoses is a 3-axis model that structures diagnostic information in a coherent and clinically relevant way. Each axis represents a crucial facet of the patient's profile, allowing the AI to contextualize data and find hidden patterns.
